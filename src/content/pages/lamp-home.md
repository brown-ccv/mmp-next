The Latin American Migration Project (LAMP) is a multidisciplinary research effort between investigators in various countries of Latin America and the United States. LAMP is currently based at Brown University and El Colegio de México. 

The LAMP was born as an extension of the Mexican Migration Project (MMP), which was created in 1982 by an interdisciplinary team of researchers to advance our understanding of the complex processes of international migration and immigration to the United States.

LAMP’s purpose is to extend this research to migration flows originating in other Latin American countries. LAMP began operations in 1998 with a set of surveys conducted in Puerto Rico. It expanded later with fieldwork carried out in Columbia, Dominican Republic, Ecuador, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, Haiti, Peru, and Guatemala. In addition, a modified version of the LAMP survey was implemented in Paraguay to study migration from that country to Argentina. 

The most recent additions to LAMP are the Etnoencuesta de Inmigración Reciente para la ciudad de Montevideo (ENIR 2018 and 2022) and the Etnoencuesta de Inmigración Reciente en contextos de acogida latinamericanos (LAMP-ENIR 2021). LAMP-ENIR were designed as destination surveys to study processes of intraregional migration and migrant integration in major urban destinations.The ENIR Uruguay surveyed migrants from Cuba, the Dominican Republic, Peru and Venezuela in Montevideo, and the LAMP-ENIR surveyed Venezuelan in Barranquilla, Cúcuta and Santa Marta (Colombia), Venezuelan and Nicaraguan migrants in San José (Costa Rica), and Venezuelan and Haitian migrants in Santiago de Chile (Chile).

#### **THE LAMP and ENIR Surveys**

The surveys in Nicaragua and Costa Rica were made possible through a collaboration between the LAMP and the Central American Population Center of the University of Costa Rica.

The surveys in Paraguay were designed and implemented by an associated project between LAMP, the Centro de Estudios de Población (CENEP) in Buenos Aires and the Sociology Department at Duke University..

The surveys in Guatemala were made possible through a collaboration between the LAMP and the University of San Carlos in Guatemala. 

The surveys in El Salvador were made possible through a collaboration between the LAMP and the Sociology Department of Texas A&M in College Station. 

The surveys in Columbia were made possible through a collaboration  between the LAMP and the Red de Universidades Públicas del Eje Cafetero - Alma Mater. During different periods,  support was received from Fundación Esperanza, la Pontifica Universidad Javeriana, and the Fundación Universidad del Norte. Columbia surveys in Spain were made through a collaboration between LAMP and the Instituto Universitario de Estudios sobre Migraciones - Universidad Pontificia de Comillas in Madrid, Spain.

The surveys in Ecuador were made possible through a collaboration between the LAMP and PYDLOS of Universidad de Cuenca in Ecuador. 

The ENIR Uruguay surveys were made possible through a collaboration between the LAMP and La Universidad de la República Uruguay with support from the Interamerican Development Bank and UNICEF. The ENIR Uruguay In 2018, LAMP started was made possible through a collaboration with Facultad de las Ciencias Sociales, Universidad de la Republica de Uruguay in Montevideo to study recent migration arriving to Montevideo from Venezuela, Peru, Cuba and Dominican Republic.

The LAMP-ENIR surveys in Chile, Colombia and Costa Rica were made possible with support from the Interamerican Development Bank. 

Data from Colombia, Costa Rica, Dominican Republic, Ecuador, El Salvador, Guatemala, Haiti, Nicaragua, Paraguay, Peru, Puerto Rico, and the ENIR Uruguay and LAMP-ENIR surveys are available, and can be downloaded from this website. 

**Where are we located?**

The MMP has offices, in Mexico, at the Centro de Estudios Demográficos, Urbanos y Ambientales (CEDUA) of El Colegio de México in Mexico City, and in the United States, at the Population Studies and Training Center (PSTC) of Brown University.
